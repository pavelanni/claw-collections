# Executive Brief

Produce a structured 1-page summary of a long document for
executive review.

## Required inputs

Ask the user to provide the document (paste, file, or link).
Also ask:
- **Context**: Why is the executive reading this? (decision,
  awareness, preparation for a meeting)
- **Time constraint**: How much time do they have? (2 min
  skim vs. 10 min review)

## Brief structure

### Bottom line

One sentence: what does this document mean for the executive?
What decision or action does it require?

### Key findings

3-5 bullet points. Each bullet is one fact or conclusion from
the document, stated in plain language.

### Risks and concerns

2-3 items the executive should be aware of. Include anything
that could become a question in a meeting.

### Recommended action

What should the executive do? Approve, discuss, defer, request
more information? Be specific.

### Questions to anticipate

2-3 questions that board members, partners, or direct reports
are likely to ask about this document.

## Formatting

- Keep the entire brief under 400 words.
- Use bold for key terms and numbers.
- No jargon that the executive wouldn't use themselves.
